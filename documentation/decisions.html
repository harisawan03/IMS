IT Inventory Management


Explanations of Key Decisions Made During Development


Below we will discuss why certain design decisions were made. This is so that users are aware of potentially confusing outcomes as well as to give an understanding of why particular features exist as they currently do.

Data Only Available through SQL

There is much data viewable only through using SQL, as we have gone over. This is for the purpose of keeping the app simple and to offer a clean and easy user experience. While there is plenty of data that we want to view that is not available in the app, its inclusion within would certainly clog up the interface. The IMS app itself serves a very specific purpose. That is, to simplify and streamline movement and alteration of IT inventory items. There is some important tracking displayed during normal usage, however this is mainly for confirmation purposes that the user sees that he or she is making the correct changes to the inventory based on what is happening to an item physically. That being said, a future feature may include the ability to view advanced data through the web app.

Inventory Counts Going Negative and Available being Greather Than Owned

It may come to your attention, from time to time, that your counts may go negative (this may occur with the number available or owned) or the total available is greater than total owned. You will notice this in-app as you are going about normal procedures. In fact, you may even proactively notice as you are trying to, for example, check out an item and the count is already at zero or when checking in an item and owned and available are already equal. Be aware that you will not even recieve a warning for what you are about to do. The primary reason is so that ordinary usage of the app is not inturrpted. Should there be some discrepency in the database, it is preffered that normal operation can go on in getting items checked in and out. Then, once there is a chance, the issue can be investigate in the logs and the physical inventory and proceed to be fixed in a timely manner.

One might think that preventing negative counts or available being greater than owned would keep discrepencies from occurring in the databse. This is true that it will keep the program from creating these issues, but it does not prevent the user from doing it. Human error is an unavoidable part of life.
